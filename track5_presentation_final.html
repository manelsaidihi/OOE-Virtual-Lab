SITUATION

Agent LOID is trapped behind the final door. Guard YURI patrols the corridor. The bare peephole gives only a narrow view (~5°, typical ref. value) — not enough.

SOLUTION

Insert a diverging lens (f' = −10 cm) into the peephole. It bends wide-angle rays inward, creating a virtual image visible over a dramatically wider field of view (~160°, typical ref. value for commercial peephole lenses).

OBJECTIVE

Calculate the lens power, image position, magnification, image height, and explain the wide-angle principle.

Q1 — OPTICAL POWER

DEFINITION

P = 1 / f'

f' must be in metres (m)

CALCULATION

f' = −10 cm = −0.10 m
P = 1 / (−0.10)
P = −10 D
⚠ Negative → DIVERGING lens

Rays diverge as if coming from a virtual focal point F at 10 cm in front of the lens.

Q2 — IMAGE POSITION (OA')

THIN LENS EQUATION

1/OA' − 1/OA = 1/f'

CALCULATION

1/OA' = 1/f' + 1/OA
1/OA' = 1/(−10) + 1/(−50)
1/OA' = −5/50 + (−1/50) = −6/50
OA' = −8.33 cm
⚠ OA' < 0 → VIRTUAL image

Image forms on the same side as the object — it cannot be projected on a screen but is clearly seen by the eye.

Q3 & Q4 — MAGNIFICATION & IMAGE HEIGHT

Q3 — MAGNIFICATION M

M = OA' / OA
M = (−8.33) / (−50)
M = +0.167
✓ M > 0 → Erect (upright) image
⚠ |M| < 1 → Reduced (smaller) image

Guard's image is upright and approximately 6× smaller than reality.

Q4 — IMAGE HEIGHT

A'B' = M × AB
A'B' = 0.167 × 1.80 m
A'B' = 0.30 m = 30 cm

The 1.80 m guard appears only 30 cm tall through the lens — a 6× reduction.

Q5 — WHY DOES THE FIELD OF VIEW WIDEN?
✗ WITHOUT LENS — Bare hole (narrow FOV)
Only rays nearly parallel to the axis pass through. Wide-angle rays miss the aperture entirely — the guard at the edges is invisible.
Note: The ~5° figure is a typical reference value for a bare hole of ~1 cm diameter at standard viewing distance. The exact angle depends on aperture size — it is not derived from this problem's data.
✓ WITH DIVERGING LENS — Widened FOV
The lens bends outward rays inward through the aperture. A compressed virtual image of the whole wide scene reaches the eye — dramatically wider coverage!
Note: The ~160° figure is a typical reference value for commercial peephole lenses. The exact FOV depends on aperture diameter and lens geometry — it is not derived from this problem's data.
